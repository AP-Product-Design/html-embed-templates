By Mesfin Fekadu, AP Music Writer · April 18, 2026

Taylor Swift's 11th studio album arrives weighed with expectation — a meditation on public heartbreak that manages to feel both sprawling and intimate. At 31 tracks across two discs, it asks a great deal of the listener's patience.

The album's first disc leans into the confessional mode that Swift has perfected over two decades, while the second introduces experimental textures that feel genuinely new for the artist.

Whether the whole is greater than the sum of its parts is a question the album raises but doesn't fully answer. What isn't in question is Swift's ability to command attention — and on this record, she demands it at full volume.
